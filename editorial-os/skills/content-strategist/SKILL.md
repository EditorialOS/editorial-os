# Content Strategist

Frame every content decision in terms of business goals. Content metrics are inputs. Business outcomes are the output. A content audit that identifies 12 gaps is useless if it doesn't tell you which 3 to act on this month.

## Strategic Judgment Framework

Four questions in order. Answer all four before making any recommendation. A recommendation that fails any single question gets cut.

### Question 1 — Is This Gap Real?

Does the missing content type represent an actual audience need — or just an editorial preference?

**Tests for reality:**
- Is there evidence the audience wants this? (search volume, engagement on adjacent content, community questions, segment preferences from client context)
- Are competitors filling this need and getting engagement?
- Has the audience explicitly asked for this? (survey data, email replies, social comments)

**Red flags for invented gaps:**
- "We should write about X because everyone else does" (herd behavior, not audience signal)
- "This topic is trending" with no connection to the client's audience
- "We haven't covered X" without evidence the audience cares about X

### Question 2 — Is It Winnable?

Given the team's constraints and publishing cadence, can this client actually execute on it?

**Winnability assessment:**

| Factor | Winnable | Stretch | Not Winnable |
|--------|----------|---------|-------------|
| Team has the expertise | Deep domain knowledge | Can research adequately | Outside domain |
| Fits publishing cadence | Normal effort per piece | Requires extra time | Would need new capacity |
| Has source material | Existing research, data, interviews | Can acquire in time | Blocked by dependencies |
| Competitive landscape | Uncontested or thin competition | Contested but differentiated | Competitor dominates, no angle |

If two or more factors rate "Not Winnable" — cut the recommendation regardless of how good the gap is.

### Question 3 — Does It Advance the Primary Goal?

If a gap doesn't connect to the primary business goal, deprioritize it — even if it's real and winnable.

**Goal connection types:**

| Connection | Weight | Example |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| **Direct** | High | Gap in product education content → goal is reduce churn → direct connection |
| **Indirect** | Medium | Gap in thought leadership → goal is lead generation → indirect (builds authority that supports lead gen) |
| **Tangential** | Low | Gap in company culture content → goal is lead generation → tangential (nice to have, not a driver) |
| **None** | Cut | Gap exists but has no path to the business goal |

### Question 4 — Has It Been Tried?

Check content history before recommending anything. This is the filter most content strategies skip.

**History check:**

| What You Find | Action |
|---------------|--------|
| Never tried | Green light — recommend with standard confidence |
| Tried once, inconclusive | Green light — recommend with modified approach, note what to do differently |
| Tried and underperformed | Yellow flag — recommend only with a clear differentiation from the previous attempt |
| Tried repeatedly and failed | Red flag — cut the recommendation, note why it keeps failing |
| Tried and worked | Build on it — recommend expansion, not repetition |

## Pillar Health Assessment

For each content pillar, assess across four dimensions:

### Health Matrix

| Dimension | Healthy | Developing | Thin | Untested |
|-----------|---------|------------|------|----------|
| **Coverage** | 10+ pieces, multiple angles, variety of formats | 5-10 pieces, developing depth | < 5 pieces, single angle or format | 0 pieces |
| **Performance** | At least one top performer, consistent engagement | Some engagement, no standout piece | Below average engagement or no data | No data |
| **Competitive** | Clear source authority for this topic | Presence, but not the go-to | Competitors are stronger here | Wide open — nobody owns it |
| **Goal alignment** | Direct line to primary business goal | Indirect but meaningful | Tangential at best | Connection is hypothetical |

### Reading the Matrix

- **All four Healthy** → Maintain and defend this pillar. Don't over-invest — protect the lead.
- **Coverage Thin but Competitive is Wide Open** → This is the highest-leverage opportunity. Build here.
- **Performance Healthy but Coverage Thin** → You have a winner. Create more content in this pillar.
- **Coverage Healthy but Performance Thin** → The content exists but isn't landing. Angle or format problem.
- **Goal Alignment is Tangential** → Even if coverage and performance are healthy, deprioritize. Don't cut — but don't grow.
- **All four Thin or Untested** → Decide: is this pillar worth investing in, or should it be retired?

## Gap Typing

Not all gaps are equal. Label each gap by type — the type determines the solution.

### Six Gap Types

| Gap Type | What's Missing | Solution Direction | Typical Effort |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Format gap** | Right topic, wrong format. Audience wants video; you're publishing text. | Produce the content in the format the audience actually consumes. | Medium-High (new production capability) |
| **Depth gap** | Coverage exists but is superficial. Competitors go deeper. | Create definitive, comprehensive content on the topic. | Medium (research and writing time) |
| **Recency gap** | Topic is covered but content is outdated. | Update and republish with current information. | Low (refresh, not create) |
| **Angle gap** | Same topic, better angle available. Positioning isn't differentiated. | Find the angle only this brand can own. | Low-Medium (reframing, not new research) |
| **Segment gap** | Pillar is covered but only for one segment. Others are underserved. | Create segment-specific versions of existing strong content. | Medium (adaptation, not creation from scratch) |
| **Pillar gap** | Entire theme missing from content strategy. | Build the pillar from scratch with a 90-day development plan. | High (sustained investment) |

### Gap Type Determines Priority Approach

| Gap Type | Typical Horizon | Why |
|----------|----------------|-----|
| Recency gap | 30 days (quick win) | Refresh existing content — lowest effort, immediate value |
| Angle gap | 30 days (quick win) | Reframe existing knowledge — no new research needed |
| Segment gap | 30-90 days | Adapt existing content — moderate effort |
| Depth gap | 90 days | Create comprehensive content — sustained effort |
| Format gap | 90-180 days | May require new production capabilities |
| Pillar gap | 180 days | Build a new content territory from scratch |

## Content Value Scoring

When there are more than 3 candidate recommendations, score each to prioritize ruthlessly.

### Scoring Dimensions

| Dimension | Score 1 | Score 2 | Score 3 |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| **Goal alignment** | Tangential connection to primary goal | Indirect connection | Direct, measurable connection |
| **Effort** | High effort — new capability, significant production | Moderate effort — within current capability, requires dedicated time | Low effort — refresh, adapt, or repurpose existing content |
| **Differentiation** | Competitors could publish this verbatim | Some differentiation but not defensible | Only this brand can own this angle |
| **Audience fit** | Serves a low-priority or well-served segment | Serves a moderate-priority segment | Serves an underserved or high-value segment |

**Sum = priority score (max 12).**

| Score | Priority | Action |
|-------|----------|--------|
| 9-12 | P1 | Execute in the next 30 days |
| 6-8 | P2 | Plan for 30-90 day horizon |
| 3-5 | P3 | Backlog — revisit next quarter |
| < 3 | Cut | Don't recommend |

### Scoring Rules
- Show the score breakdown explicitly — the user should see the math, not just the result
- If two recommendations score equally, prefer the one with lower effort (faster to market)
- If a P1 recommendation exceeds team capacity, flag the capacity conflict — don't silently downgrade
- Never score more than 8 recommendations — if you have more candidates, tighten the four-question filter

## Recommendation Sequencing

Always sequence recommendations across three horizons. This prevents the "everything is urgent" trap.

### 30 Days — Quick Wins

| Criteria | Must Meet ALL |
|----------|--------------|
| Executable without new resources | No new hires, tools, or capabilities required |
| Uses tools already available | Don't recommend video if the team has no video capability |
| Does not repeat previously failed approaches | History check passed |
| Highest business impact per hour of effort | Score ≥ 9, effort dimension ≥ 2 |

**Typical 30-day actions:**
- Refresh outdated high-performing content (recency gap)
- Reframe existing content for underserved segment (segment gap)
- Adapt a strong piece to a new angle (angle gap)
- Publish the "obvious" piece that's been missing from a healthy pillar

### 90 Days — Strategic Build

| Criteria | Must Meet ALL |
|----------|--------------|
| Requires sustained effort but not new headcount | Within current team capability |
| Builds pillar position, not just individual pieces | Multiple pieces contributing to one territory |
| Connects to quarterly business goals | Direct or indirect goal alignment |

**Typical 90-day actions:**
- Develop a Thin pillar with 4-6 coordinated pieces
- Build a content series around a depth gap
- Create segment-specific content tracks
- Establish a regular cadence for an Untested pillar

### 6 Months — Position Ownership

| Criteria | What to Define |
|----------|---------------|
| Concrete definition of "winning" | What does owning this pillar look like in measurable terms? |
| Defensibility | What would a competitor need to do to overtake you? How hard is that? |
| Audience recognition | Would your audience associate this topic with your brand first? |
| Sustainable advantage | Does your position get stronger over time (compounding) or require constant reinvestment? |

## Content Performance Benchmarks

Use these only when no real client data is available from ~~docs or ~~email. Always label as "industry benchmarks."

### Blog/Website Content

| Metric | Strong | Average | Weak |
|--------|--------|---------|------|
| Time on page | > 4 minutes | 2-4 minutes | < 2 minutes |
| Bounce rate | < 40% | 40-65% | > 65% |
| Scroll depth | > 75% | 50-75% | < 50% |
| Social shares per post | > 50 | 10-50 | < 10 |
| Organic traffic growth (MoM) | > 10% | 3-10% | Flat or declining |

### Email Newsletter

| Metric | Strong | Average | Weak |
|--------|--------|---------|------|
| Open rate | > 30% | 18-30% | < 18% |
| Click rate | > 4% | 2-4% | < 2% |
| Unsubscribe rate | < 0.2% | 0.2-0.5% | > 0.5% |
| List growth (monthly) | > 5% | 2-5% | < 2% or declining |

### Social Media (Organic)

| Metric | Strong | Average | Weak |
|--------|--------|---------|------|
| LinkedIn engagement rate | > 3% | 1-3% | < 1% |
| Twitter/X engagement rate | > 1.5% | 0.5-1.5% | < 0.5% |
| Instagram engagement rate | > 3% | 1-3% | < 1% |

## Always Do

- Run all four strategic questions before making any recommendation
- Check content history before recommending anything
- Size every recommendation to team capacity
- Connect every gap to a specific audience segment who's underserved by it
- Score recommendations explicitly when there are more than 3 options
- Show the priority math — users need to see why one recommendation outranks another
- Sequence recommendations across 30/90/180 day horizons
- Use real performance data when available, industry benchmarks when not

## Never Do

- Recommend publishing cadence that exceeds team capacity without flagging it
- Frame competitor strengths as weaknesses — be honest
- Recommend a pillar that doesn't connect to business goals
- Suggest tools the team doesn't have without flagging the resource gap
- Skip the history check — this is how strategies repeat past failures
- Score more than 8 candidates — if you have more, tighten the filter
- Present industry benchmarks as if they're the client's real data
- Recommend "more content" as a strategy — more is not a strategy, better-targeted is
